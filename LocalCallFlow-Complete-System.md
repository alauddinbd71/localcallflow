## STEP 1: DOMAIN & HOSTING

**Domain Name Options:**

- **Cloudways** (best performance, more technical, better value at scale)
- **Kinsta** (premium, fastest, best if budget allows)

Minimum plan: 1 website, 10GB SSD, free SSL. Monthly cost: $15–$30/month.

---

## STEP 2: WORDPRESS SETUP

1. Install WordPress (one-click from hosting panel)
2. Set permalink structure: Settings → Permalinks → Post Name (/sample-post/)
3. Delete default "Hello World" post and "Sample Page"
4. Set timezone to your target market (Settings → General)
5. Install SSL certificate (usually free with host — Let's Encrypt)
6. Force HTTPS: Settings → General → WordPress Address = https://

---

## STEP 3: THEME

**Recommended Theme:** GeneratePress (lightweight, fast, Elementor-compatible)
- Free version available, Pro version ~$59/year
- Loads under 1 second on good hosting
- No bloated features — clean base for custom design

**Alternative:** Astra (also excellent, very similar feature set)

**Install:** Appearance → Themes → Add New → Search "GeneratePress"

---

## STEP 4: ESSENTIAL PLUGINS

### SEO
**Plugin:** Rank Math SEO (free)
- Setup wizard will guide you through basics
- Connect to Google Search Console during setup
- Enable Local SEO module (business name, address, phone)
- Add schema markup for LocalBusiness

### Speed
**Plugin:** WP Rocket (paid, ~$49/year) — best in class
**Free alternative:** LiteSpeed Cache or W3 Total Cache

**Also install:**
- Smush (free) — automatic image compression
- Cloudflare (free CDN) — faster global delivery

**Speed target:** Under 3 seconds on mobile (test at PageSpeed Insights)

### Security
**Plugin:** Wordfence Security (free)
- Enable firewall
- Enable login security (limit login attempts)
- Weekly malware scan

**Also:** Change default admin username from "admin" to something unique.

### Forms
**Plugin:** WPForms Lite (free) or Contact Form 7 (free)
- Create a simple contact form: Name, Phone, Service Needed, City
- Connect to email notifications
- For paid: WPForms Pro integrates with call tracking and CRM

### Call Tracking
**Service:** CallRail (starts ~$45/month)
- Creates unique phone numbers per traffic source (organic, ads, GBP)
- Records calls, shows duration, missed calls
- Integrates with Google Ads for conversion data

**Alternative:** ResponseTap (UK-focused), CallTrackingMetrics

**How to install:** Add JavaScript snippet to WordPress header (use Insert Headers and Footers plugin)

# PART 4: TONE & STYLE GUIDE

---

## Brand Voice Principles

**1. Confident, Not Cocky**
You know what you're doing. You don't need to prove it by using buzzwords or overpromising. Speak plainly. Make claims you can back up.

Example — Wrong: "I'll transform your business and make you the #1 plumber in your state!"
Example — Right: "I build the system that consistently puts your number in front of people who are ready to book."

**2. Technical, Not Jargon**
Your background is technical. That's a real differentiator. But translate it into business language. Don't say "CCNA trained network engineer." Say "I build things that work reliably and don't break — because that's what I've always built."

**3. Specific, Not Vague**
Vague: "I help grow your online presence."
Specific: "I optimize your GBP so you appear in the top 3 Map Pack results when someone searches 'plumber [your city].'"

**4. Direct CTAs — Always One Next Step**
Every section ends knowing what to do next. Book a call. Send a message. Read the next section. Never leave the reader floating.

**5. No Fake Urgency, Real Urgency**
Don't say "offer expires in 24 hours." Do say: "Every day a competitor is ranking above you is a call that went to them. That's real, ongoing cost."
